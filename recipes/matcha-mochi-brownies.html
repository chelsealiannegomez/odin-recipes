Matcha Mochi Brownies

Matcha Mochi Brownies are a delicadent matcha-flavored cross between mochi and brwonies. Because of the glutinous rice used, it has a more chewy and bouncy center. Recipe from https://www.eatingwithkirby.com/2017/05/easiest-matcha-mochi-brownie-recipe.html

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons high quality matcha powder
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2.25 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 0.5 teaspoon baking soda
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1.25 cup sugar
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F/175°C.
  2. Combine water, butter, oil, and matcha powder in a pot over medium heat. Make sure to whisk the matcha powder until there are no lumps and everything has melted together.
  3. Take it off the heat.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine all of the dry ingredients (2.25 cups of glutinous rice flour, 1.25 cups of sugar, a pinch of salt, and .5 tsp of baking soda).
  5. Pour the matcha liquid mixture into the dry ingredients. Stir to combine.
  6. Add the eggs, vanilla extract and mix until well combined. Make sure to combine evenly and that there are no lumps.
  7. Pour the mochi brownie batter into an oil sprayed or parchment lined pan. Remove any air bubbles by knocking the pan a few times on the counter.
  8. Bake in the oven for 45-50 minutes (or until toothpick comes out clean).
  9. Remove from the oven and let cool (10 minutes) before cutting into even squares.
